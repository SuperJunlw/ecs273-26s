The latest analyst update on McDonald's includes a modest fair value adjustment from US$344.85 to US$345.00, a move that sits alongside a wave of Street target changes across the name. Analysts raising or trimming targets into the mid US$300s, including moves to US$325, US$354, US$360 and US$385, are using these shifts to debate how much of the long term growth and earnings story is already reflected in the share price. Read on to see what is driving these moves and how to keep track of the evolving narrative around McDonald's.
Stay updated as the Fair Value for McDonald's shifts by adding it to your watchlist or portfolio. Alternatively, explore our Community to discover new perspectives on McDonald's.
Tigress Financial lifted its McDonald's target to US$385 from US$360, pointing to the global brand, unit expansion, AI driven efficiencies, and the asset light franchise model as key supports for the long term equity story.
KeyBanc, JPMorgan, Guggenheim, Mizuho, Truist, RBC, UBS, Baird, Barclays, Citi and others have raised targets into the US$300s, reflecting confidence in McDonald's execution, including digital investment and momentum in the U.S. business highlighted by KeyBanc.
Erste Group, BTIG and Argus upgraded the shares earlier in the year, with Erste citing high profitability and what it describes as good prospects, and Argus pointing to digital platform spending and new product launches.
More recent commentary includes a downgrade from Erste Group after its earlier upgrade, which signals that at least one prior bull is reassessing the risk reward balance at current levels.
Target tweaks such as the US$1 reduction at Morgan Stanley and the US$9 cut at KeyBanc indicate that some analysts see less upside at current prices, even while maintaining generally constructive views on the business model.

McDonald's is reported to be launching a lower priced value menu in April, featuring items at $3 and below and new $4 breakfast meal deals aimed at reinforcing its value positioning.
The company is also preparing additional deals alongside the value menu rollout, providing customers with more entry price points at the lower cost end of the quick service market.
McDonald's is reportedly planning to add energy drinks to its menu, expanding its beverage range beyond soft drinks and coffee to cater to demand in the energy drink category.
Under the share repurchase program announced on February 10, 2025, McDonald's bought back a total of 6,653,726 shares for $2,016.35m, including 1,829,426 shares for $560.48m between October 1, 2025 and December 31, 2025.
Fair value moves slightly from US$344.85 to US$345.00 per share.
Revenue growth assumption shifts from about 5.87% to about 5.84%.
Net profit margin assumption adjusts from about 33.17% to about 33.27%.
Future P/E multiple changes from about 29.36x to about 29.33x.
Discount rate moves from 8.97% to about 9.01%.
