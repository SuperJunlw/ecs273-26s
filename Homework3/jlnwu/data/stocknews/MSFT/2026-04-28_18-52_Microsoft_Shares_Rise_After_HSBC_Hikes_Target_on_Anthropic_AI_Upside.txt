Microsoft Corporation (NASDAQ:MSFT) may get a lift from deeper ties with Anthropic as it prepares to report fiscal third-quarter results on April 29, HSBC said in a Tuesday note.
The bank said the Microsoft story still hinges on Azure, but added that new AI relationships could support the cloud unit's outlook, according to a Tuesday note from HSBC.

HSBC estimated Anthropic's annual revenue run rate climbed to about $30 billion in April from more than $9 billion in December. It said Microsoft agreed in November to invest $5 billion in Anthropic, while Anthropic committed to buy $30 billion of compute capacity from Microsoft.
The Microsoft note also said more deals like that could follow, which may help reduce the company's reliance on OpenAI. HSBC said OpenAI accounted for about 45% of Microsoft's remaining performance obligation, which stood at $631 billion in the second fiscal quarter of 2026.
HSBC kept a Buy rating on Microsoft and raised its price target to $593 from $588. The firm said Azure remains the main growth engine and could post a 33.6% compound annual revenue growth rate through 2030.
Shares of MSFT rose about 1% on Tuesday afternoon.
