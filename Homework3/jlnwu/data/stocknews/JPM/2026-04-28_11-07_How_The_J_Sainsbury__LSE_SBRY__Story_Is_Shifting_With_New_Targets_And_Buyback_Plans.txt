J Sainsbury’s consolidated price target has shifted slightly, with fair value now set at £3.55 compared with £3.52 before. This small move reflects the mix of more optimistic analysts, who point to potential valuation upside, and more cautious voices highlighting execution risks that keep the overall Street view fairly balanced. As you read on, you will see how these differing opinions and the latest research help shape an evolving narrative you can track over time.

Deutsche Bank has raised its J Sainsbury price target twice in recent weeks, including a 5 GBp increase on 27 April 2026, which points to some support for the current valuation case.
JPMorgan lifted its price target by 13 GBp on 24 April 2026, which suggests the firm sees room in the shares relative to its previous view.
Citi raised its target by 17 GBp on 20 March 2026, which adds another example of upward revisions feeding into the consolidated £3.55 fair value.
Citi has also downgraded J Sainsbury as of 28 April 2026, signaling that, despite a higher target level in March, the risk reward balance now looks less attractive to that team.
Goldman Sachs has issued a downgrade as well, indicating concerns around execution or growth delivery that keep some investors cautious even with recent target increases from other firms.
