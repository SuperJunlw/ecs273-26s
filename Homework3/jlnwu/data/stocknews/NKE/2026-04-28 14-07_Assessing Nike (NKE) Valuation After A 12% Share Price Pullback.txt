NIKE (NKE) is back in focus after recent share price weakness, with the stock down about 12% over the past month and around 29% over the past 3 months, which is prompting fresh attention from valuation minded investors.

That recent 12% 30 day share price pullback and the 28.7% year to date share price return, alongside a 19.3% 1 year total shareholder return decline, suggests momentum has been fading rather than building.

With NIKE now trading at a discount to the average analyst price target and carrying a low value score, the key question is whether the recent weakness signals an undervalued global brand or if the market is already pricing in future growth.
According to the most followed narrative on Simply Wall St, NIKE's fair value of $87.90 sits well above the recent $45.14 share price, which sharply frames the current pullback.
Where Will Nike Be in 5 Years? Nike will likely remain the dominant global sportswear brand, but it faces increasing competition. Revenue growth will depend on its ability to scale DTC, innovate in performance and lifestyle, and expand internationally.
