Analysts have updated their fair value estimate for UnitedHealth Group to US$386.08, compared with the prior US$364.63. This puts a fresh number on what they see as central value for the stock. This shift comes as recent research has mixed optimism and caution, with some firms lifting targets and others trimming them based on views around regulation, margins, and how the stock should be priced against those risks. As you read on, you will see how these moving targets shape the story you are trying to follow and what to watch next.
Stay updated as the Fair Value for UnitedHealth Group shifts by adding it to your watchlist or portfolio. Alternatively, explore our Community to discover new perspectives on UnitedHealth Group.
Multiple firms including TD Cowen, Raymond James, Piper Sandler, Truist, Deutsche Bank, BofA, RBC Capital, Morgan Stanley, Oppenheimer, Baird, Barclays, Evercore ISI, Jefferies and Bernstein have recently raised their UnitedHealth price targets, which signals more constructive views on execution and long term earnings power relative to earlier expectations.
Morgan Stanley naming UnitedHealth a Top Pick and Erste Group and HSBC issuing upgrades point to continued interest in the stock as a core health care holding. Analysts emphasize the scale of the platform and diversified business mix when thinking about valuation.
Argus upgrading shares after what it called a strong Q1 suggests some on the Street see the company’s recent performance as supportive of current research models and fair value work.
Cantor Fitzgerald describing the January selloff as overdone indicates that at least one firm views prior market reaction as more severe than its own assessment of long term fundamentals and regulatory risk.
Mizuho, Truist, JPMorgan, RBC Capital, UBS, BofA and Leerink all reduced price targets earlier in the year, highlighting concerns around Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services decisions, medical cost trends and potential pressure on margins.
Deutsche Bank describing a CMS rate notice as a significant negative for managed care and Cantor Fitzgerald saying a quick recovery for health insurers after the CMS call is unlikely underscore the regulatory overhang that several firms continue to factor into their valuations.
