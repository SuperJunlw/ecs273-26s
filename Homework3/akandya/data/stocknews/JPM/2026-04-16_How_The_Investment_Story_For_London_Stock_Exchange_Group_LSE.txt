JPMorgan has adjusted its model for London Stock Exchange Group, lifting the price target by £1.00 to £122.54. This signals refreshed conviction in the current valuation framework rather than a wholesale change in view.
The revised target reflects updated analyst assumptions on how the business may execute on its plans. In JPMorgan's view, the existing share price still has room to reflect those assumptions more fully.
The relatively small size of the JPMorgan revision hints at ongoing caution, with the bank fine tuning numbers instead of making a large shift that would imply a stronger change in expectations.
JPMorgan's careful adjustment underlines that, while the stock is supported by its current investment case, there are still questions around execution and how quickly potential drivers may be reflected in earnings.

Elliott Investment Management has built a significant stake in London Stock Exchange Group and is pressing for a full portfolio review, including the data business, exchange operations and the 51% stake in Tradeweb Markets, as well as a potential sale of the bond trading arm.
Elliott is calling for £5b of share buybacks over the next 12 months following a 33% share price fall over the past year, and has issued statements around LSEG’s 2025 full year results suggesting there is scope for additional actions to narrow the valuation gap with peers.
LSEG has partnered with Anthropic and others as part of 10 new plug ins aimed at business customers, with LSEG contributing capabilities for AI powered workflows in areas such as investment banking and wealth management.
The group launched Model as a Service, a governed marketplace for financial institutions to host, distribute and analyse models, with Societe Generale providing fixed income, FX, ESG and equities datasets and integration into partners’ AI ecosystems via Model Context Protocol connectors.
Fair value moved from £122.32 to £122.54, representing a very small upward adjustment in the modelled estimate.
The revenue growth assumption shifted from 6.11% to 6.29% for long-term pound sterling based sales.
The net profit margin assumption moved from 16.65% to 17.03% for expected profitability on pound sterling earnings.
The future P/E multiple moved from 36.74x to 35.84x on projected earnings.
The discount rate increased from 8.69% to 8.73% in the required return used in the analysis.
