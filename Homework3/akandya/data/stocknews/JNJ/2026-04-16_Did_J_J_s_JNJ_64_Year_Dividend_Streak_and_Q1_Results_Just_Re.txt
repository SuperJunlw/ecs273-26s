Title: Did J&J's (JNJ) 64-Year Dividend Streak and Q1 Results Just Redefine Its Investment Narrative?
Ticker: JNJ
Published: 2026-04-16T14:07:05
URL: https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/did-j-js-jnj-64-130705288.html?.tsrc=rss

Earlier this week, Johnson & Johnson reported first‑quarter 2026 results showing sales of US$24,062 million and net income of US$5,235 million, and its board approved a 3.1% increase in the quarterly dividend to US$1.34 per share, marking 64 consecutive years of dividend growth.
At the same time, management highlighted momentum in newer medicines and MedTech innovations, reinforcing the company’s shift toward higher-growth therapies as older products face biosimilar competition.
With Johnson & Johnson raising its dividend again, we’ll now examine how this renewed payout growth affects the company’s existing investment narrative.

To own Johnson & Johnson today, you need to believe it can replace revenue from older drugs like STELARA with newer therapies and MedTech innovations, while continuing to fund a growing dividend. The latest quarter’s strong sales growth and another dividend increase support that view, even as net income declined and biosimilar pressure remains a key near term risk. For now, the dividend hike itself does not materially change the company’s main catalyst or risk profile.
The dividend increase to US$1.34 per share, marking 64 straight years of growth, is the most relevant update here because it ties directly into J&J’s appeal as a steady income name. Management paired that higher payout with raised 2026 sales and earnings guidance, underpinned by momentum in oncology, immunology and MedTech. Together, those announcements reinforce the idea that newer products and devices could help support ongoing dividend growth despite headwinds from loss of exclusivity.

Compared with the baseline focus on STELARA erosion, this more bullish stance highlights how differently you might weigh pipeline potential versus concentration risk, and recent results could shift those expectations in either direction.
