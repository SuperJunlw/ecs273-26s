Title: How NVIDIA Optics Deal and New AI Facility Will Impact Lumentum Holdings (LITE) Investors
Ticker: NVDA
Published: 2026-04-17T01:17:05

Lumentum Holdings recently announced multiyear agreements with NVIDIA, a very large US$2 billion investment in advanced optics, and plans for a new 240,000-square-foot Greensboro, North Carolina facility to produce indium phosphide-based optical devices for AI data centers.
These moves highlight Lumentum’s push to become a core supplier to hyperscale AI infrastructure, with expanded domestic manufacturing aimed at supporting long-term demand from large cloud customers.
Next, we’ll examine how these NVIDIA agreements and new Greensboro capacity could reshape Lumentum’s existing AI-driven investment narrative.

To own Lumentum today, you need to believe advanced optics will remain central to AI data center buildouts and that the company can turn its hyperscale traction into durable, profitable growth. The NVIDIA agreements and US$2.0 billion investment sharpen that story by supporting capacity and technology, but they also heighten concentration risk around a small group of customers and execution risk on new fabs, which remain the key near term swing factors for the stock.
The Greensboro, North Carolina facility announcement feels especially important here, because it links directly to the NVIDIA optics deal and to Lumentum’s capacity constraints risk. Bringing 240,000 square feet of indium phosphide manufacturing online is core to supporting hyperscale AI orders and could influence how quickly Lumentum can respond if demand or product mix shifts around its biggest cloud customers.
