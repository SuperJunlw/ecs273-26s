Alphabet, parent of NasdaqGS:GOOGL, is in talks with the U.S. Pentagon to deploy Google Gemini AI models for classified defense uses.
The potential agreement would mark a major expansion of Alphabet’s role in government and defense work.
The discussions focus on how Gemini could support sensitive military applications while addressing ethical and policy safeguards.
For Alphabet, known primarily for Google Search, YouTube and cloud services, deeper work with the Department of Defense would signal a new phase in how the company approaches public sector clients. The focus on Gemini, its family of generative AI models, highlights that government demand is broadening beyond basic cloud contracts into higher value AI capabilities.
If the Pentagon deal goes ahead, investors may pay closer attention to how Alphabet sets boundaries on use cases, especially around surveillance, targeting and decision making in conflict zones. The outcome could influence how other government agencies and global allies think about adopting Gemini, and how NasdaqGS:GOOGL competes for future large scale public sector AI contracts.
