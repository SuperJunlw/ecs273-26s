A Discounted Cash Flow, or DCF, model projects a company’s future cash flows and then discounts them back to today’s value using a required return, giving an estimate of what the business might be worth per share.
For UnitedHealth Group, the model used is a 2 stage Free Cash Flow to Equity approach, based on cash flow projections in $. The latest twelve month free cash flow is about $15.8b. Analyst estimates and subsequent extrapolations point to projected free cash flow of $27.8b in 2030, with a series of annual projections between 2026 and 2035 that Simply Wall St discounts back to today using its own assumptions.
Bringing all those discounted cash flows together produces an estimated intrinsic value of about $816.74 per share. Compared with a current share price around $314, the DCF output suggests the stock trades at a 61.5% discount to this intrinsic value, which indicates that UnitedHealth Group is screened as materially undervalued on this model alone.

For profitable companies, the P/E ratio is a useful yardstick because it ties what you pay per share directly to the earnings that support that price. Investors usually look for a P/E that lines up with the company’s growth outlook and perceived risk, with higher growth or lower risk often justifying a higher multiple.
UnitedHealth Group currently trades on a P/E of 23.6x. That sits above the Healthcare industry average of 22.8x and above the peer average of 20.3x. On these simple comparisons, the shares are priced at a higher earnings multiple than many peers.
Simply Wall St’s “Fair Ratio” for UnitedHealth Group is 36.3x. This is a proprietary estimate of what the P/E might reasonably be, given factors such as earnings growth profile, profit margins, industry, market capitalization and company specific risks. Because it pulls these elements together in one metric, the Fair Ratio can be more informative than a straight comparison with peer or industry averages, which do not fully reflect differences in growth or risk. With the Fair Ratio above the current 23.6x P/E, this approach suggests the shares are trading below what that earnings based framework would indicate.
