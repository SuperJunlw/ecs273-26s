Title: Is Cheniere Energy the LNG Stock to Buy on Mideast Tension?
Ticker: CVX
Published: 2026-04-16T13:21:00
URL: https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/energy/articles/cheniere-energy-lng-stock-buy-122100543.html?.tsrc=rss

Rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East are once again reshaping the global energy markets, particularly the liquefied natural gas (“LNG”) landscape. Supply disruptions in key exporting regions, combined with shipping risks through critical routes like the Strait of Hormuz, have tightened global LNG availability. This has created a favorable backdrop for U.S.-based exporters like Cheniere Energy LNG, which stands to benefit from sustained international demand and higher realized prices. While companies like Venture Global VG and Chevron CVX also operate in the LNG space, Cheniere’s positioning as a leading U.S. exporter gives it a unique edge in capitalizing on current market dynamics.
The ongoing instability in the Middle East is contributing to a structural tightening of LNG supply. Disruptions in Qatar — one of the world’s largest LNG exporters — along with shipping uncertainties, have reduced the available global supply. At the same time, Europe’s gas storage levels remain significantly below normal, forcing early and aggressive restocking ahead of winter. This combination is pushing international LNG prices higher, even as U.S. natural gas prices remain relatively subdued.Unlike oil, LNG requires specialized infrastructure for transport, which limits how quickly supply can respond. This creates a strong incentive for buyers in Europe and Asia to secure long-term contracts from reliable suppliers like Cheniere Energy. While Venture Global continues to expand its modular LNG operations and Chevron leverages its large-scale projects in Australia, Cheniere Energy’s established export infrastructure positions it as a key supplier in this tightening market. In particular, the company’s scale and contract visibility stand out.
Despite favorable industry dynamics, Cheniere’s stock performance has been lackluster. Shares of the company are up about 13% over the past year, significantly trailing Venture Global, which has gained more than 37%, and Chevron, which is up 36.6%. This divergence suggests that while investors recognize the broader natural gas export opportunity, they may already be pricing in stronger growth expectations for VG and the diversified strength of CVX.
Image Source: Zacks Investment Research
However, this relative underperformance could also present an opportunity. While Venture Global’s rapid expansion and Chevron’s integrated model continue to attract investors, Cheniere Energy offers a more focused exposure to U.S. LNG exports. The current geopolitical environment could help narrow this performance gap if earnings continue to improve.
Cheniere’s earnings outlook remains solid, supported by favorable pricing and expanding capacity. The Zacks Consensus Estimate projects earnings growth of 27% in 2026, followed by another 10% increase in 2027. This growth is driven not only by higher LNG prices but also by improved operational efficiency and expanding export capacity.
Image Source: Zacks Investment Research
The company’s financial performance already reflects these strengths. In 2025, Cheniere reported strong gains, with revenues rising sharply and net income increasing significantly, supported by higher realized prices and disciplined cost management. According to its latest presentation, adjusted EBITDA reached nearly $6.9 billion for the year, highlighting strong operating leverage. In other words, Cheniere Energy offers a balance of growth visibility and operational stability, compared with Venture Global’s aggressive expansion strategy and Chevron’s diversified earnings base.
Image Source: Cheniere Energy
From a valuation standpoint, Cheniere looks interesting. The stock is priced at a discount to Chevron on a forward price-to-earnings basis but commands a premium relative to Venture Global. This reflects its established market position and stable cash flows, supported by long-term contracts covering the majority of its capacity.
Image Source: Zacks Investment Research
While VG’s lower valuation may appeal to growth-oriented investors, and CVX offers diversification and dividend stability, Cheniere Energy provides a more direct play on global LNG demand. Its highly contracted business model reduces exposure to short-term price volatility, though it may limit upside during periods of extreme price spikes. Still, compared with Venture Global and Chevron, Cheniere’s balance of stability and growth remains compelling.
Cheniere Energy is well-positioned to benefit from ongoing Middle East tensions and tightening global LNG supply. Strong demand from Europe, combined with supply disruptions in key regions, supports a favorable pricing environment. Cheniere’s scale, contract visibility and expanding capacity provide a clear pathway for sustained earnings growth. However, with the stock already reflecting some of these positives and lagging in recent performance, upside may be more measured in the near term. Cheniere Energy stock currently carries a Zacks Rank #3 (Hold), suggesting investors may want to wait for a more attractive entry point while monitoring global LNG market developments.
